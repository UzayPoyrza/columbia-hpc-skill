# Quarterly Maintenance Schedule for Columbia HPC Clusters

Source: https://columbiauniversity.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/rcs/pages/1282539535/Quarterly+Maintenance+Schedule+for+Columbia+HPC+Clusters

---

Beginning in March 2026, maintenance will occur **four times per year**. Each scheduled window is a standardized three-day period from **Tuesday through Thursday of the second full week of the month**.

|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Quarter** | **Month** | **Maintenance Window** |
| Q1 | March | **March 10–12, 2026** (Tuesday–Thursday) |
| Q2 | June | **June 9–11, 2026** (Tuesday–Thursday) |
| Q3 | September | **September 8–10, 2026** (Tuesday–Thursday) |
| Q4 | December | **December 8–10, 2026** (Tuesday–Thursday) |

## Purpose

Regular quarterly maintenance allows the HPC team to perform necessary updates and upgrades in a controlled and predictable manner. This reduces the risk of unplanned downtime.

Maintenance activities typically include:

- Firmware updates
- Operating System (OS) patching
- Networking infrastructure updates
- Storage system updates and upgrades

## Impact on Users and Communication

These quarterly Tuesday-Thursday windows are reserved in advance to ensure you have ample time to plan your workflows and adjust long-running jobs around the potential downtime.

### Communication and Duration

- The duration of these planned outages varies. Specific, confirmed dates and times will be announced to cluster users in advance via listserv.
- Outage details will also be posted to the [CUIT Service Alerts](https://cuitalerts.columbia.edu/cuitalerts?id=service_status_cu&service=5a29f67e13b9560006c376022244b09c) platform.
- **Contingency:** While these maintenance periods are reserved on the calendar, we may not utilize every window if no updates, patches, or upgrades are required during that cycle.

### Cluster Access During Outage

- **Cluster Unavailability:** All HPC clusters will be unavailable for login and job execution during the confirmed outage window.
- **Job Management:** Jobs will not start if their estimated runtime overlaps with the confirmed maintenance period. Please plan job submissions accordingly to ensure they complete *before* the start of the maintenance.

## Urgent/Emergency Maintenance

While we strive to use the quarterly schedule, urgent or emergency maintenance may occasionally be required outside of these four planned dates.

Examples of urgent maintenance include:

- Faulty hardware replacement (e.g., failed disks)
- Critical storage system issues
- Other time-sensitive repairs

When emergency maintenance is necessary, we will provide as much advance notice as possible and work diligently to minimize the impact on active research and running jobs.

## Questions and Support

If you have questions about these maintenance windows or anticipate that your current research workflows may be affected, please contact the HPC Support team at hpc-support@columbia.edu.
