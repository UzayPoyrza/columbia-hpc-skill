## Getting Help

The High-Performance Computing (HPC) team provides technical support, software configuration, and troubleshooting for all Columbia University researchers and students.

**Primary Support Channel:** [hpc-support@columbia.edu](mailto:hpc-support@columbia.edu) *Please include your UNI, the cluster name (e.g., Insomnia, Ginsburg), and any relevant Job IDs in your request.*

## Support Tiers

Support priority is determined by the participant tier. All users should contact the support email (hpc-support@columbia.edu) for assistance.

| **Tier** | **Priority Level** | **Scope of Support** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Purchasers** | High | Full technical support & software consulting. |
| **Renters** | High | Full technical support & software consulting. |
| **Educational** | Standard | Course-related software and cluster access. |
| **Free Tier** | Low | **Self-service only.** Support is limited to [Free User Documentation](https://columbiauniversity.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/rcs/pages/62144271/Free+HPC+Cluster+User+Documentation). |

## Service Level Expectations

We strive to resolve issues as quickly as possible. Our response times are categorized by the severity of the problem.

### **Standard Response Time**

For **Less Critical Problems** (e.g., application software errors, single execute server downtime, or configuration requests), CUIT staff will respond within **two (2) business days**.

### **Business Hours**

Support is provided during standard University business hours:

- **Monday – Friday: 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM**
- *Excluding University holidays.*

### **System Alerts & Status**

If a critical system-wide issue occurs, CUIT will notify users via:

1. **Email notifications** from the cluster listserv.
2. [**CUIT HPC Service Alerts**](https://cuitalerts.columbia.edu/cuitalerts?id=service_status_cu&service=5a29f67e13b9560006c376022244b09c) (for major outages).
